Why I don't watch romance anime anymore

go back

13/8/25

Back when I was 14-17 romance anime was my favourite thing to watch. I’d scavenge from the best of the best and would always try to find ones with a satisfying conclusion. Granted these are few and far between. The ones that did ended up at the top of my list’s rankings and the ones that didn’t at the bottom. This is the entire reason why I got into manga in the first place, to be able to see these stories through. The first manga I actually read and finished was Sankarea. Not because the anime was particularly dear to me but when I made up my mind about starting to read manga to get the full stories of these shows that was the one that first crossed my mind. I never made a list, but also don’t think I ever got around to reading everything I set out to read. Probably a combination of getting lazy, discovering better anime and manga and just realizing that the romance anime I was reading wasn’t really that worth it to begin with. It didn’t help that my first exposure, Sankarea, left probably more of an open ending than the show did, but I digress.

Most of my favourites IPs would be romance or have romance tints to it: Horimiya, Angel Beats, Hyouka, Kokoro Connect, Oregairu, Toradora, Kaichou wa maid-sama, Clannad, Yamada-kun to nananin no majo, the list just goes on and on. I vividly remember getting my anime recommendations from shitty “top 10 romance anime” or simply “anime kiss amv”. I’d watch so many shitty shows just cuz I saw one scene with a kiss or anything that vaguely resembled progress.

Looking back, during this time I was really only interested in the romantic comedy: funny characters, funny misunderstandings, lighthearted romances, characters finally get together, end. I think the reason was that I was projecting what my teenager-hormones-through-the-roof self wanted to experience. This is also part of the reason why I think I could never get behind more “serious” romance stories that dealt with more serious topics and had more character development. The reality is that I’ve never experienced an actual meaningful romantic relationship, so whatever commentary these would provide on the topic would go straight over my head.

The other kind that I actually used to enjoy doesn’t do it for me anymore either. Reason being simply that I don’t want to watch other people get together while I’m sitting here alone. Probably comes off as bitter and small, but that’s how I feel about it. I’m at a point in my life where most people I know are in a relationship. I’ve had hangouts where I was the only single person in the room (one funny example was when I was sitting in a round table, 3 couples to my left and 3 couples to my right, literally the odd one out). Put it plainly, it’s already been rubbed on my face so many times that I’d rather not come home from that put on a show to relax and have the same thing happen to me again. All the people I hang out with are great by the way, this is just a me thing.

There were points in my life that I was desperate about getting into a relationship, but now I don’t care about it anymore. Everything that I’m about to say could and should be considered cope since it’s coming from a 20 something yo male, but I don’t have time for one at the moment, plus I’ve made changes in my life for the better and that give me things to be preoccupied with. One of the reasons for the aforementioned desperation was that I wasn’t content with my everyday life in the first place. I’d project inner problems like these into more external problems like jobs, grades, relationships, etc.

At some point I thought I had developed some sort of fear of intimacy, but unfortunately I think my mental health is too straight to be that interesting. I think I’ve just always been too shy to commit to getting involved with someone. This is another reason why I said earlier that I’m not desperate about it anymore, because while my younger self would love to blame others, I’ve come to realize that this is a me problem. I’m more socially reserved than most and stopped talking to people just because I couldn’t think of anything else to say. Never been great at small talk. It is also true that if someone is really interested in you they’ll talk to you even if nothing of interest is being discussed, but I’m getting off track. It 's complicated.

Point being, I don’t watch romance anime or manga anymore because 1) I’m missing a core part of the human experience that would make me understand and appreciate them 2) I don’t wanna get reminded of it.


18/8/25: Addendum #1

Yesterday while walking back from a dinner birthday party where I again was the only single person on the table I felt this weird feeling. I wouldn’t say I wanted to cry, cuz I wasn’t sad. I was emotional I guess? Not sure how to describe it, but it felt like that one time I cried while walking back from a nightclub (story for another time), but way less dramatic. Thankfully it wasn’t as cold as it was when I was going to the restaurant. But I desperately wanted to get home. Even so I didn’t rush, I’d say I was walking slower than average, humming to myself the two songs I know how to sing. I don’t know how much that adds to anything really, but it’s not the first time I’ve felt this while hanging with this group. I’ve felt this way while hanging out with other people too, but not for a long time. Nowadays it’s just with this group. I guess I felt alienated, even though I wasn’t by any stretch and it’s very unfair of me to say that, I still felt it. I don’t really know what to do with this information.
